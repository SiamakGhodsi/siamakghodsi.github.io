About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science (CS) working on both theoretical and applied aspects of Machine Learning at the Free University of Berlin under the supervision of Prof. Eirini Ntoutsi. I am also a member of the L3S Research Center in Hannover, Germany. My PhD has mainly contributed to trustworthy, safe, and responsible AI with a focus on bias discovery and mitigation. A main part of my PhD path started as an early-stage researcher (ESR) of the amazing network, Marie Skłodowska-Curie granted EU project, NoBIAS-ITN (2021-2024). Also, due to my compassion for optimization problems and applied AI/ML science, in parallel to my PhD studies, I have now started a new research position in the Human Decision project; DFG-SPP program 2443. We research how to optimize modular (dis/a)ssembly systems to enhance efficiency, support the circular economy, and care for explainability & trust of these systems through hubrid neuro-symbolic decision support systems. My research interests lie in the following topics:

  • Graph learning & graph Theory
  • (Un/Semi)supervised learning: Clustering and Social Networks, Representation Learning
  • Trustworthy ML: bias, fairness, interpretability, and privacy
  • Multi-objective optimization: fairness-accuracy trade-off, time-cost-efficiency, ...
  • Recommendation systems: multi-stakeholder fairness, popularity bias, cold-start, sparsity, and scalability
  • Data imbalance: skewness toward a specific class or group
  • Transformers: LLMs, Attention mechanism, Graph-transformers, Zero/Few-shot Learning, Fine-tuning
  • Neurosymbolic AI: Toward Agentic SLMs, Knowledge Distillation
