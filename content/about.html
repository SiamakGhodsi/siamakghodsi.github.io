About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the Free University of Berlin, supervised by Prof. Eirini Ntoutsi, and a member of the L3S Research Center in Hannover, Germany. My research focuses on trustworthy, safe, and responsible AI, with particular emphasis on bias discovery and mitigation.

I was an Early-Stage Researcher (ESR) in the Marie Sk≈Çodowska-Curie-funded NoBIAS-ITN project (2021-2024), where much of my doctoral work took root. Currently, I also hold a research position in the Human Decision project (DFG-SPP 2443), where we optimize modular (dis)assembly systems to enhance efficiency, support the circular economy, and develop hybrid neuro-symbolic decision support systems with explainability and trust at their core.

Research Interests:

  • Graph learning & graph theory
  • (Un/semi)supervised learning: clustering, social networks, representation learning
  • Trustworthy ML: bias, fairness, interpretability, and privacy
  • Multi-objective optimization: fairness-accuracy, time-cost-efficiency tradeoffs
  • Recommendation systems: multi-stakeholder fairness, popularity bias, cold-start challenges
  • Data imbalance and class/group skewness
  • Transformers: LLMs, attention mechanisms, graph transformers, few-shot learning, fine-tuning
  • Neurosymbolic AI: agentic SLMs, knowledge distillation
